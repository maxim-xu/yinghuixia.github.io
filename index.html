Frequentist or Bayesian?

Frequentist: the truth is drawn with a high probability in a sequence of repetitions of the same experiment. The underlying variable is fixed but unknown, while in Bayesian inference, the unknown variables are random and it is possible to assign a probability associated with the unknowns.

Frequentist approach accepts or rejects a hypothesis testing (with a p-value characterizing the probability of obeserving a result as extreme as the test statistics). Bayesian approach gives a probability distribution for the observed paramenters.
